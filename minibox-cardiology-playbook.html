Selling PFTs into a cardiology practice

The cardiologist does not want to be a pulmonologist. They want an answer to a question their patient already handed them. Sell the answer, not the machine.

Get in their head

Why a cardiologist actually tests

Every reason maps to a patient already on their schedule. Lead with the one that fits the practice in front of you.

Trigger 01

The breathless patient

Shortness of breath is the symptom the heart and lungs share. A complete study runs about fifteen minutes and tells the cardiologist which organ to treat, instead of treating the heart and hoping.

Trigger 02

The amiodarone patient

Baseline diffusion capacity is recommended before amiodarone, and almost nobody gets it. Every atrial fibrillation practice is sitting on scripts with no baseline. This is a safety gap you can close.

Trigger 03

The heart failure patient

A quarter to half of heart failure patients also have COPD, and about half of that is undiagnosed. Finding it changes how they dose beta blockers and bronchodilators.

Handle the pushback

Objection battlecard

Tap a question to open the answer. These are the six things a sharp cardiologist will fire back.

+I am a cardiologist, not a pulmonologist. This is not my lane.
You are not diagnosing lung disease for its own sake. You are answering the question your patient already brought you: is this breathlessness the heart or the lungs. A complete study runs about fifteen minutes and tells you which, so you treat the right organ the first time. When it is clearly pulmonary, you refer with data instead of a guess.
+Who runs it? My staff is already maxed out.
A medical assistant runs it after a short training. The MiniBox coaches the patient through the maneuver and flags quality in real time, so you do not need a respiratory therapist. The complete test takes about fifteen minutes, or ten if you run spirometry and diffusion capacity without lung volumes, and it happens during the visit the patient is already in your chair for.
+Will I actually get paid, or will these get denied?
These are established codes with clear medical necessity when the patient has documented breathlessness or is on a drug like amiodarone. The device produces the report and interpretation that support the claim, and we give you the covered diagnosis list and documentation templates so claims go out clean. This is getting paid for work you are already indicated to do.
+I do not read PFTs all day. What if I misinterpret one?
Every study can go to a board certified over read, so a signed interpretation comes back to you. You get the clinical answer and the medicolegal cover without becoming a pulmonologist. Over time most cardiologists read the common patterns themselves and keep the over read for the harder ones.

Why testing belongs in cardiology

The differential

Is the breathlessness the heart or the lungs

Dyspnea is the symptom both organs produce. Without a lung measurement you treat the heart and hope. About a third of heart failure patients are mislabeled with COPD they do not have, and a similar share carry airflow obstruction nobody has confirmed. Both change how you dose beta blockers and inhaled therapy.

The amiodarone gap

Protect the patients you already prescribe it to

Guidelines recommend pulmonary function testing with diffusion capacity at baseline before amiodarone, because a falling diffusion capacity is the earliest sign of lung toxicity. Adherence to that recommendation is very low, mostly because the test lives somewhere else. Put it in your office and the baseline actually happens.
