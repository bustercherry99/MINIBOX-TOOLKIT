## PART 1 — THE HOOK (open by naming Salesforce yourself, before he can)

Avi, I already know what you are going to say. We have Salesforce. We already paid for the system that does this.

And you are right, we have it. Here is the truth though. Nobody uses it.

*(let that sit for a second)*

Not because your people are lazy. Because it does not work the way a rep actually works. The account data is in there, but there is so much of it, so much overlap, so many duplicate accounts, that finding the one thing you need takes longer than just winging it. So the reps wing it. They have all built their own little systems, their own notes, their own spreadsheets, and now the real picture of this company is scattered across a hundred places and lives in nobody's head.

Here is the part that should bother us both. The information is there. We are paying for it. And it is worthless, because if you cannot find it, it does not exist. Data you cannot find is not data. It is just noise you paid for.

Every hour a rep spends digging through Salesforce for something they cannot find is an hour they are not selling, and an hour they are not home with their family. That is the cost. Not the license fee. The time.

What I have been building attacks that directly. We are entering a world where you do not search anymore. Everything is just there, the second you need it. That is the whole game.

## PART 4 — THE ARRIVAL (this is the heart of it, slow down)

A rep pulls up to an account.

The toolkit already knows where they are, so the second they arrive, the account is on the screen. No searching. No digging through Salesforce in the parking lot. Before they walk in, they see everything. The last time this office ordered. The last service issue and whether it got resolved. Every device in that building by serial number. And the one that makes money: every warranty, calculated automatically.

Because every serial number has a date attached, the system does the math on its own. It knows the exact day each device goes out of warranty. Six months out, it fires an alert. "This account has a device coming off warranty in six months." Now the rep is not walking in to say hello. They are walking in with a reason and money on the table that would have otherwise slipped by.

*(let it land, then go bigger)*

Now stretch that across the whole territory. The system is doing that math for every account, every device, every warranty, all the time. So it answers the question every rep actually wants answered: where is the low hanging fruit. Where is the money. Where should I spend my time this week.

It learns the territory. It sees which accounts are heating up, which devices are aging out, where the next dollar is easiest, and it points the rep straight at it. Nobody digs. Nobody guesses. The tool hands it to them.

And to be clear about what this is and is not. This is not us watching where reps drive. This is the tool working for them. It uses where they are to pull up what they need. The rep is not being monitored, the rep is being armed.

## PART 5 — EVERYTHING FLOWS (the back door, tie it to money)

Everything I just described flows into the rep's hand. There is also a back door that flows the other way, into the business.

Right now a rep with a receipt has to log into some separate system to deal with it. We pay for tools like that. Why. Let the rep snap a photo with their phone, straight into the toolkit, and it flows into finance on its own. No second app. No monthly fee for a thing our own system can do. Same for mileage, tolls, whatever they touch in a day. One tool in, one tool out. The rep stops juggling five apps, and the company stops paying five bills.

## PART 7 — THE BUSINESS MODEL SHIFT (the big-picture reason it matters to him)

Here is what all of this really buys you, Avi, and it is bigger than any one rep.

Once the information is finally usable, once you can actually see what is happening across every account and every territory in real time, you can steer the whole business off of it. You can see what is moving, what is dying, where the market is going, and you can shift the model to match it. Right now those shifts are almost impossible to make because the picture is too scattered to see. Give the company one clear picture, and you can turn the wheel before the market forces you to.

That is the difference between running the business and reacting to it.

## PART 8 — HOW WE ROLL IT OUT (make it feel safe and phased)

I am not asking you to flip a switch on the whole company tomorrow. This goes in stages, and each stands on its own.

**Phase 1. The toolkit, live.** It already exists and the reps already use it. Done.

**Phase 2. The memory.** Connect the accounts, orders, service history, serial numbers and warranty dates. Now when a rep pulls up, the account and the warranty math are already on the screen. This is the arrival experience, the biggest quality of life jump for the rep.

**Phase 3. The hunt.** The system points at the low hanging fruit and the warranty alerts fire on their own. The tool is not just remembering, it is finding money.

**Phase 4. Everything flows.** The back door goes in, expenses and assets flow through one place, and we retire the subscriptions we no longer need. The toolkit becomes the single system the whole company runs through, and you get the one clear picture to steer by.

Every phase delivers on its own. If we stopped after Phase 2, every rep would already walk into every account fully loaded and nobody would be searching for anything.

## APPENDIX — OBJECTION CARDS (do not read these, know them cold)

**"We already have Salesforce / a CRM for this."**
Right, and be honest, how much of the team actually lives in it. The problem was never that we lacked a system. It is that the system is too hard, so the data goes stale and nobody trusts it. I am not replacing Salesforce with another thing to log into. I am building the thing that is easy enough that reps use it without being told to, which is the only way the data ever becomes real. Salesforce can even sit underneath it. The point is the rep never has to go dig in it again.

**"This sounds like surveillance / the reps will hate being tracked."**
It is the opposite. This tool works for the rep. It uses their location to pull up their account so they walk in ready, not to check up on them. Reps will fight to keep this, not fight against it, because it saves them the two hours a day they spend hunting for information.

**"This is a huge build, how do we know it works."**
It already works. The toolkit is live and in their hands today. That was the hard part and it is done. Everything else is connecting things we already have into one place.

**"What does this cost us."**
Less than we spend now. It replaces subscriptions we already pay for. *(then go to the savings number.)*
