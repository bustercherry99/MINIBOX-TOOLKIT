Why cardiologists run PFTs
👨‍⚕️
Sort cardiac vs. pulmonary dyspnea
Complete the first-line workup on the same visit
›

Cardiac and pulmonary disease are the likeliest causes of shortness of breath. Spirometry is a recommended first-line test in the chronic-dyspnea workup — so the pulmonary half no longer has to wait for an outside referral.

Source: Syed Z. “Chronic Dyspnea: Diagnosis and Evaluation,” American Family Physician, 2020;101(9):542–548.
aafp.org/pubs/afp/issues/2020/0501/p542.html
💓
Untangle heart failure and COPD
Overlapping disease that hides in older patients
›

When spirometry is the diagnostic tool, COPD is found in roughly 25–50% of heart failure patients — and it’s severely underdiagnosed. A PFT helps separate and co-manage the two.

Nuance we’ll always state plainly: HF congestion can itself cause a reversible obstruction, so spirometry helps distinguish and co-manage — it isn’t a standalone COPD diagnosis.

Sources: HF & COPD review (PMC12250576); Airflow obstruction in stable systolic HF, ~39% obstructive pattern (PMC5219786).
PMC12250576  ·  PMC5219786

Diffusing capacity: where heart disease shows up in the lungs

The full-PFT difference

A plain spirometer measures airflow. A complete PFT adds lung volumes and DLCO — diffusing capacity — which reflects the gas-exchange surface and pulmonary vasculature, exactly where cardiac disease affects the lungs.

In pulmonary hypertension due to HFpEF, nearly half of patients have a DLCO below 45% predicted, and reduced DLCO is associated with higher mortality. DLCO ranks among the key prognostic markers in pulmonary vascular disease alongside 6-minute walk distance and NT-proBNP.

Sources: JACC: Heart Failure, 2016 — Diffusion capacity & mortality in PH due to HFpEF; DLCO prognostic value in PH (PMC6935895).
jacc.org — DLCO & mortality in HFpEF-PH  ·  PMC6935895

Stratify peri-procedural risk
Baseline lung function before cardiac procedures
›

Abnormal pulmonary function is associated with more postoperative pulmonary complications after cardiac surgery. A PFT gives a baseline that helps stratify risk — not a pass/fail gate, but useful signal before a procedure.

Sources: PFT in preoperative high-risk patients, Perioperative Medicine 2024 (PMC10913451); Spirometry & postoperative complications in cardiac surgery (PMC7682508).
PMC10913451  ·  PMC7682508
💊
Monitor cardiotoxic therapy & drug-induced PH
A targeted surveillance role
›

The 2022 ESC cardio-oncology guidelines address monitoring for drug-induced pulmonary hypertension (notably with dasatinib). This is a narrower, drug-specific surveillance role — valuable for the right patients rather than routine screening.

Source: 2022 ESC Guidelines on Cardio-Oncology, European Heart Journal 2022;43(41):4229–4361.
