DLCO Gas System 0
Gas analyzer, regulator, valve, tank
▼
DLCO measurements unavailable / Gas Analyzer error
Device displays "Gas Analyzer error" or hangs on "Preparing for new DLCO measurement." CO or CH4 analyzer failure. Manual confirms this requires PulmOne support or device replacement.
High
Rapid DLCO gas tank depletion
E-tank runs out in days or within a week. Prior valve replacement did not resolve. Suspected internal manifold or regulator leak. Chronic in devices 7+ years old.
High
DLCO calibration failing repeatedly
Weekly DLCO calibration check with 3L syringe failing to return values within 0 ± 1 mL/min/mmHg. Indicates gas analyzer drift or partial valve issue.
Medium
CO / CH4 low concentration readings
DLCO CO and CH4 show abnormally low values despite confirmed gas supply connection and adequate pressure (4 to 5 bar secondary). Gas analyzer degradation likely.
Medium
🔧
Occluder Valve / Interrupter 0
Fast interrupter valve, LVM shutter, flow interruption
▼
Valve stuck in closed or open position
No test results displayed. Manual specifically lists this as a failure mode requiring device replacement via PulmOne support. Valve is the core fast interrupter mechanism for LVM and LVC tests.
High
Valve not opening fast enough (slow actuation)
LVM test completes with repeated alerts: "not enough qualified valve closure events." Patients report feeling resistance or delay at breath interruption. Valve actuator is degrading.
High
LVM tests failing repeatedly / inconsistent results
Acceptable LVM maneuvers cannot be obtained across multiple patients. May indicate partial valve degradation before full failure. Staff reports device "never used to do this."
Medium
💻
Tablet / Control Unit 0
Battery, screen, software, OS compatibility
▼
Battery degradation / physical case damage
Battery swelling, gas expansion within the battery causing the tablet case cover to separate. Immediate safety issue. Device must be replaced. Seen in units 5+ years old under heavy daily use.
High
Tablet losing charge rapidly / not holding power
Device requires near-constant wall connection. Tablet no longer holds charge between patients. Battery cycle life exhausted. Replacement tablet available from PulmOne.
Medium
Software update no longer supported on current OS
MiniBox software cannot be updated. Older Dell tablets may be locked out of Windows updates required for current MiniBox software versions. Leads to compliance and performance gaps.
Medium
Touchscreen unresponsive or sluggish
Staff uses keyboard or mouse to navigate because touch is unreliable. Normal wear on aging Surface/Dell units. Impacts daily workflow and testing speed.
Low
🔌
CPU Cable / Connectivity 0
USB data cable, HHU connection, communication
▼
CPU cable causing DLCO / communication errors
"Communication with device has been lost" or Gas Analyzer error triggered by bending or flexing the main USB CPU cable. Cable outer coating cracking or brittle. Full DLCO failure results from cable flex.
High
HHU disconnects during testing
Handheld unit loses connection intermittently. Sessions interrupted mid-test. Cable from HHU to main unit shows wear, cracking, or poor seating at connector panel.
Medium
USB ports not recognizing peripherals
Flash drives for database export, printers, or keyboards not recognized consistently. Internal USB port degradation over years of daily use.
Low
📊
Flow Sensor / Calibration 0
Bidirectional flow sensor, pressure, accuracy
▼
Daily calibration check failing repeatedly
Calibration check with 3L syringe fails despite proper technique. Manual states if multiple consecutive calibrations fail after thorough procedure, the PulmOne representative must be contacted. Results may already be clinically inaccurate.
High
FVC values drifting from expected / outlier results
Returning patients showing unexpected drops in FVC or FEV1 not explained clinically. Sensor drift in aging bidirectional flow sensors can produce clinically misleading results before outright failure.
Medium
Warm-up time increasing or inconsistent
Device taking longer than 10 minutes to reach thermal stability or requiring more frequent restarts for zeroing. Temperature sensor degradation over time.
Low
🫁
Handheld Unit (HHU) / Main Body 0
Physical unit, locking pin, chassis, power system
▼
HHU not seating / locking properly
Handheld unit no longer locks securely to the main device. Locking pin or groove shows wear. Unit wobbles or disengages during docked DLCO testing. Creates test quality and safety issues.
High
Blue power light flickering or off despite power connected
Blue indicator light around HHU connection unreliable. May indicate fuse issue or internal power supply degradation. Manual lists fuses as a cause of device not working.
Medium
ON/OFF switch stiff or unreliable
Main power switch becoming difficult to operate or not switching cleanly. Manual specifically lists switch stuck in ON or OFF as known failure mode. Functional but a leading indicator.
