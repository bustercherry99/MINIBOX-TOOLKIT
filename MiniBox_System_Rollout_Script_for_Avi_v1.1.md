## PART 1 — THE HOOK (open cold, no warmup)

Avi, before I show you anything, I want to ask you one question.

When one of our reps walks into an account, do they know what they are walking into? The last time that office ordered. The last time a device had an issue. What serial numbers are sitting in that building and which ones are about to fall out of warranty.

*(pause, let him answer)*

They do not. Not because they are not good. Because that information is scattered across a dozen tools that do not talk to each other, and half of them we pay a monthly subscription for. The rep is walking in blind, and the answers exist, they are just not in their hand.

I have been building the thing that puts it in their hand. And I think it changes how this entire company runs.

---

## PART 2 — WHAT YOU ARE LOOKING AT TODAY (hand him the iPad here)

*(Open the toolkit on the iPad and put it in his hands. Let him tap around while you talk.)*

This is the MiniBox Toolkit. It is live, it works, and it is what the reps carry into every office.

Everything a rep needs is in here. The quote builder that mirrors our Salesforce quote. The ROI calculator. The financing tab. The billing helper. The forms, the ACH, the RMA, all signable on the spot. The training. The account map.

*(let him tap, then take the moment)*

Here is the part I want you to hear. This is not the product I am pitching you. This is the front door.

---

## PART 3 — THE TURN (this is the whole pitch)

Right now this tool helps a rep in the moment and then forgets everything. The rep closes it and the information is gone.

What I am building is a tool that remembers. One system, one brain, that every account and every rep feeds into. And because it remembers, it can start doing the rep's homework for them before they ever ask.

Let me show you what that actually looks like on a normal day.

---

## PART 4 — THE ARRIVAL (this is the heart of it, slow down)

A rep pulls up to an account.

The toolkit already knows where they are, so the second they arrive, the account is on the screen. Before they even walk in the door, they see everything. The last time this office ordered. The last service issue and whether it was resolved. Every device in that building by serial number. And here is the one that makes money: every warranty, calculated automatically.

Because every serial number has a date attached to it, the system does the math on its own. It knows the exact day each device goes out of warranty. So six months out, it fires an alert. "This account has a device coming off warranty in six months." Now the rep is not walking in to say hello. They are walking in with a reason, a renewal, a conversation, money on the table that would have otherwise slipped by unnoticed.

*(let that land, then go bigger)*

Now stretch that across the whole territory. The system is doing that math for every account, every device, every warranty, all the time. So it can answer the question every rep and every manager actually wants answered: where is the low hanging fruit. Where is the money. Where should I spend my time this week.

It learns the territory. It sees which accounts are heating up, which devices are aging out, where the next dollar is easiest to get, and it points the rep straight at it. Nobody is guessing anymore. The tool tells you where to hunt.

And to be clear about what this is and is not. This is not us watching where reps drive. This is the tool working for them. It uses where they are to pull up what they need. The rep is not being monitored, the rep is being armed.

---

## PART 5 — EVERYTHING FLOWS (the back door, tie it to money)

Now flip it around. Everything I just described flows in one direction, into the rep's hand. There is also a back door, and it flows the other way, into the business.

Right now a rep with a receipt or an expense has to log into some separate system to deal with it. We pay for tools like that. Why. Let the rep snap a photo with their phone, straight into the toolkit, and it flows into finance on its own. No second app. No monthly fee for a thing our own system can do.

Same logic everywhere. If a rep wants to tie in their toll account, their mileage, whatever they touch during the day, it flows through the same place. One tool in, one tool out. The rep stops juggling five apps, and the company stops paying five bills.

That is the bridge to the part that matters to your bottom line.

---

Every one of those separate subscriptions is a monthly fee for a job our own system can already do.

When everything runs through one place, most of that stack becomes redundant. We are not adding a line item. We are collapsing the ones we already pay for into one.

## PART 7 — HOW WE ROLL IT OUT (make it feel safe and phased)

I am not asking you to flip a switch on the whole company tomorrow. This goes in stages, and each one stands on its own.

**Phase 1. The toolkit, live.** It already exists and the reps already use it. This is the foundation and it is done.

**Phase 2. The memory.** We connect the accounts, the orders, the service history, the serial numbers and warranty dates. Now when a rep pulls up, the account and the warranty math are already on the screen. This is the arrival experience, and it is the single biggest quality of life jump for the rep.

**Phase 3. The hunt.** The system starts pointing at the low hanging fruit across the territory, and the warranty alerts start firing on their own. Now the tool is not just remembering, it is finding money.

**Phase 4. Everything flows.** The back door goes in, expenses and assets flow through one place, and we retire the subscriptions we no longer need. The toolkit becomes the single system the whole company runs through.

Every phase delivers something real on its own. If we stopped after Phase 2, every rep would already walk into every account fully loaded.

What I am really offering is the system that runs PulmOne. One place the whole company flows through, that arms every rep the second they pull up, finds the money nobody was looking at, and saves us on day one by replacing what we already pay for.

I want to be the one who builds it. Give me the list of what we are paying for, and let me show you exactly what this replaces and what it saves.
