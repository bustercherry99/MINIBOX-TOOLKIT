How it ships

Reviewed once. Nothing goes out by accident.

The whole point: no more one-off posts written on the fly. Every graphic is drafted, approved, and queued ahead of time, so what publishes is always something we already signed off on.

Draft the batch

Decks built two weeks ahead against the approved grid, with copy and slides done together.

Compliance review

Regulatory checks codes & clinical claims. No dollar amounts, ever. Approved language is reusable.

You sign off

You see every post as it will appear before it can be scheduled. Change or kill anything.

Queue & auto-post

Approved posts loaded into the scheduler for their fixed day & time. Publishes on its own.

Measure & feed back

Weekly numbers logged; leads routed to sales. Winners inform the next batch.

The safeguard you asked for: a post can only reach the scheduler after it's been reviewed and approved. There is no path from "idea" straight to "live." The quarter is pre-loaded, so a busy week never forces a rushed, unreviewed post.
The posting engine

How it actually goes live, hands off, for the price of a coffee

Approved posts don't publish themselves out of a folder. Something has to fire them. One LinkedIn-approved scheduler holds the whole quarter and posts each one on its fixed day. Nobody logged in at 9am; no busy week ever forces a rushed post.

The tool

Publer

A LinkedIn-approved scheduler. Load the quarter once, set Tue / Wed / Fri, and every carousel publishes on its own. Bulk-loads up to 500 posts at a time, the whole 36 in one sitting.

Why not build our own

The tollbooth

Auto-posting to a company page requires LinkedIn's own approval, a multi-week review that can be denied, plus a server to run and maintain. Publer already cleared that gate and maintains it. We rent the approval instead of building the highway.

Built-in safeguard

Approval workflow

The paid tier includes a sign-off step: nothing reaches the feed until it's approved inside the tool. The governance gate above lives in the software, not just on paper.

$5/mo for one company page, billed monthly, or about $4/mo billed annually. Carousel & document scheduling, bulk loading, and the approval workflow are all included at this tier.
The one manual step, once a quarter: a person loads the approved batch into the scheduler and walks away. After that, all 36 posts fire autonomously, with no daily touch, no developer, and no LinkedIn approval wait. Total tooling cost for the full quarter: about $15.
The giveaway engine

They comment. We DM the tool. Sales gets a name.

Every keyword CTA needs something real behind it. Instead of promising a paper handout, we hand out working tools we already built, starting with the one every billing desk actually needs.

The magnet

PFT Billing Helper

Our interactive coding tool: which PFT codes to bill, which ones bundle together, which combos get denied, Medicare rules and modifiers. It already carries a built-in coding disclaimer and contains no dollar figures. A biller will use it every week with PulmOne's name on it.

The mechanic

Comment CODES → we DM the link

Comments are what LinkedIn's algorithm rewards most, so every comment pushes the post to more feeds. Keeping the link in the DM also dodges LinkedIn's penalty on posts with outside links. Ten seconds of work per reply, and every commenter is a named lead routed to sales.

The payoff

The company people look to for answers

The tool lives at a trackable link, so we can count exactly how many people grabbed it each week. Real usage, real numbers for the report, and PulmOne becomes the name on the tool the office opens every billing day.

Pending sign-off: nothing goes public until leadership and regulatory approve the tool for release. The Billing Helper is built and ready behind a private link; the giveaway switches on only after that approval. More giveaways can follow the same pattern, one per franchise.
The new look

Dark, loud, and unmistakably PulmOne

The LinkedIn feed is a wall of white. A white slide vanishes into it. These bookend-dark, cyan-lit slides stop the scroll, closer to MGC's best-performing graphic than to a plain cheat sheet.

PFT Payday · Week 1

Who's missing from your schedule?

Why this slide works, in plain terms:

  • It's dark in a white feed. Almost every LinkedIn post is white. A dark navy slide with a cyan glow is the one thing that makes a thumb stop scrolling.
  • The first line is a headline, not a list. "Who's missing from your schedule?" makes you curious; "CPT codes for PFT" doesn't. We lead with the question or the money, so people swipe to slide 2.
  • Every carousel is five short slides, each with one job: grab attention, name the problem, show a proof point, show the payoff, ask for the reply. Short enough that people actually reach the ask.
  • That glowing curve in the corner is an obstructive flow-volume loop, the scooped-out spirometry tracing of an airway that can't empty (COPD, asthma). It sits on every slide, so a physician recognizes both "these people work in pulmonary" and "this is the patient we help you catch" before reading a word. It's our signature, like a logo.

The format ladder

Start where the odds are best. Climb from there.

Not every LinkedIn format performs the same. Here they are ranked by what the platform's own 2026 data says actually earns reach and engagement, so we spend our effort where it pays. We open with the format that wins today, and grow into the rest on a schedule, not all at once.

1
Document / carousel
WE START HERE
~7% engagement · highest on the platform

The swipeable slide deck. Wins because people swipe through it, and that dwell time is exactly what the algorithm rewards, roughly 3x the engagement of video and nearly 6x plain text. Zero cost to produce: we already build these. This is the entire 36-post quarter.

2
Short vertical video
WE GROW INTO
~5.6% engagement · fastest-growing format

Under 60 seconds, captions on. Vertical video reach jumped ~80% and video views are up ~36% year over year. Builds trust a slide can't, a rep or a physician talking straight to camera. Filmed on a phone; the cost is reps and doctors, not a production budget.

3
Text post with a hook
~2% engagement · biggest raw reach

Lowest engagement rate but can hit the largest impression numbers, and 51% of users say text is what they're most likely to engage with. Nearly free. A quick hot take or customer anecdote to break up the carousels.

4
Polls
USE SPARINGLY
1.6x reach · but only 0.37x engagement

LinkedIn calls this a "reach trap", lots of eyeballs, shallow interaction. Fine now and then to spark comments ("your biggest PFT billing headache?"), never the backbone of a week.

Why not just do video now? Because carousels win today and cost us nothing, and accounts that rotate formats see ~37% more follower growth than accounts stuck on one. We bank the easy wins first, learn the filming, then layer video in, so the page keeps climbing instead of stalling.

Sit our own guys and referring physicians down for short interviews. Build a shelf of real voices we can pull up on a phone the moment we walk into a facility.

The whole point: real marketing reach without a big budget. Carousels are free to make, video is a phone and a few minutes of someone's time, and the scheduler runs about $15 a quarter. We're buying attention with consistency and real voices, not ad spend.
