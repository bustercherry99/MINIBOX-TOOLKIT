1 · The clinical guideline for rheumatology

2023 ACR/CHEST Guideline for Screening and Monitoring of ILD in Systemic Autoimmune Rheumatic Diseases

Johnson SR, Bernstein EJ, Bolster MB, Chung JH, et al. Arthritis Care & Research 2024 Aug;76(8):1070–1082.

  • Free full text: PMC12646460 (screening & monitoring)
  • Companion: PMC12646464
  • Record: PubMed 38973729

What it establishes. Diseases in scope: rheumatoid arthritis, systemic sclerosis, idiopathic inflammatory myopathies (including antisynthetase syndrome), MCTD, and Sjögren disease. SLE is explicitly excluded. The guideline conditionally recommends screening with PFTs and HRCT, and states that "PFTs should include spirometry, lung volumes, and DLCO." Bronchodilator responsiveness testing is never mentioned.

Monitoring intervals: SSc-ILD and myositis-ILD every 3–6 months in the first year; RA, Sjögren and MCTD every 3–12 months; less frequently once stable.

Be honest about the evidence strength. Of 15 graded recommendations, 14 are conditional and certainty of evidence is very low for 14 of 15. The single strong recommendation is against surgical lung biopsy. This guideline will not win an argument framed as "the guideline requires it" — it supports a clinical rationale, not a mandate.

The 94010 vs 94060 question, settled

NCCI pairs 94060 (column 1) with 94010 (column 2) at modifier indicator 0, in force since 1 January 1996. Indicator 0 means no modifier will ever unbundle them — not 59, not XU. Billing both for the same session is a hard denial of 94010.

CMS's own manual (§J.7) describes 94060 as assessing "symptoms that might be related to reversible airway obstruction" — an obstructive question. ILD is a restrictive process. The same section confirms 94060 already includes the bronchodilator administration, so 94640 cannot be billed alongside it.

Supervision definitions: 42 CFR 410.32. General supervision does not require the physician to be present. Direct supervision requires presence in the office suite (real-time two-way video now qualifies; audio-only does not).

The practical consequence: the correct ILD panel is also the easier one to staff. 94729 is a Type 1 add-on code — billed without one of its seven permitted primaries, it denies with no modifier override.

CY2026 national unadjusted, non-facility: 94010 $29.73 + 94726 $63.80 + 94729 $63.46 = $156.99. Locality adjustment applies; verify with the Look-Up Tool.

3 · Coverage policy — what changed

  • Medicare Coverage Database — search your own MAC
  • LCD L34149 — Respiratory Care (active)
  • Article A57224 — Billing & Coding: Respiratory Care (active)
The standalone PFT policies are retired. LCD L34247 and article A57216 — "Pulmonary Function Testing" — were retired 4 November 2023 and folded into Respiratory Care. Almost every online source still quotes them as live, including at least one MAC's own crosswalk page. Any frequency figure of the form "once annually" or "up to four times per year" traces to that retired document and should not be relied on.

Several MAC jurisdictions, including New York, now have no PFT-specific policy at all. That cuts both ways: no covered-diagnosis list to fail, but no safe harbour either — only the general reasonable-and-necessary standard.

The screening exclusion — the item needing a business decision

Active policy states that Medicare coverage "excludes routine (screening) tests for asymptomatic patients with or without high risk of lung disease." Statutory basis: 42 U.S.C. §1395y(a)(1)(A) and (a)(7).

The ACR/CHEST guideline recommends screening at-risk patients. Medicare does not pay for screening. No policy reconciles these, and we should not pretend otherwise.

The workable line: a patient with documented lung involvement or respiratory symptoms is being monitored — diagnostic and payable. A truly asymptomatic patient with no established lung disease is being screened — patient-liable, requiring an ABN. Documentation requirements are unchanged: an order with clinical indication, ATS-quality studies, and a signed written interpretation, with a physician signature attesting to review where the report is computer-generated.

4 · ICD-10-CM

  • CMS ICD-10-CM files · CDC/NCHS source files

Use the "with lung involvement" form

Where MAC covered-diagnosis lists exist, they carry the lung-involvement variants — M34.81 (SSc), M35.02 (Sjögren), M32.13 (SLE), M33.01/.11/.21 (myositis). The bare disease codes are absent. When ILD is established, add J84.170 or J84.178 and sequence the rheumatologic disease first — both carry an explicit "code first" instruction. J84.17 alone is not billable.

Two descriptor errors worth knowing, because both circulate: J84.112 is idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis — J84.111 is not. And M34.82 is systemic sclerosis "with myopathy," not lung involvement.

The Z79.6– family took effect 1 October 2022. Coding sites labelling it "effective October 1, 2025" are showing annual-edition boilerplate, not an origination date.

Worth raising internally. Routine PFT surveillance of the asymptomatic methotrexate patient is not supported by the evidence — a systematic review concluded the findings "do not support routine PFT for early detection" of drug-induced lung disease, and the 2021 ACR RA guideline makes no PFT monitoring recommendation. Baseline testing in a symptomatic patient or one with known lung disease is defensible. A standing test-everyone protocol is not, and we should not sell it as recurring volume.

The same code is correct in one specialty and wrong in another. That is precisely why a single shared coding page cannot serve all three, and why the Toolkit now handles them separately.

Supporting: AAFP, Office Spirometry (2020) — bronchodilator testing "applies specifically to obstructive patterns"; a restrictive pattern instead calls for full PFTs with lung volumes and DLCO. ERS/ATS 2022 interpretive standards — spirometry can only suggest restriction; lung volumes are required to confirm it.
